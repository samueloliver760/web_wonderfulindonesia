Tourist Destination

These are some recommedation place that you must visit when arrive to Indonesia !

Danau Toba

Lake toba is a large natural lake in North Sumatra, Indonesia. The lake is located in the middle of the northern part of the islands of Sumatera. The lake is about 100 km long, 30 km wide and up to 505 m deep. It's the largest lake in Indonesia and the largest volcanic lake in the world

Cape Kelayang

Cape Kelayang is located in the middle of Tanjung Kelayang Beach. It's the rock that rocks that form a unique shape simillar to a head of a bird. The locals call it Batu Garuda and became one of the best landmarks in Belitung islands.

Wakatobi Nation Park

Wakatobi Nation Park is a marine national park located in Southeast Sulawesi. Wakatobi itself is the combination of the name of four main islands (Wangi- Kaledupa-Tomia-Binongko).Since 2005, the park is listed as a tentative World Heritage Site

Cinta Islands

Cinta Islands is located in the coast of Tomini Gult in Gorontalo. Cinta Islands literally means the island of loves. If look from above or drone, the islands forms the shape of a heart as id Mother Nature sculpted the universal symbol of love. Cinta islands is located among the 23 inhabited islands at Tomini Bay.

Garuda Wisnu Kencana

Garuda Wisnu Kencana is a tourist destination and attraction located at Badung, Bali. It's devoted to the Hindu god Vishnu, and his mount, Garuda, the mythical bird who became his companion. The statue is 120,9 m tall and completed in Augusts 2018.

Borubudur

Borubudur is a Buddhist temple in Magelang, Central Java. It's the world's largest Buddhist temple consists of nine stacked platforms, six squarea nd three circular topped by a central dome. This temple built in the 9th century during the reign of Sailendra Dynasty

Raja Ampat

Raja Ampat is located in the most western part of Papua and has the area of 4,6 million hectare. Raja Ampat consits of 4 big islands which are Waigeo, Batenta, Sulaweti and Misol. Beside that, there are still 1.847 small islands around the Raja Ampat. Raja Ampat is one of the best diving destination in the world because has 75 % alive coral and many kinds of fishes.

Labuan Bajo

Labuan Bajo is located at the western end of the large islands of FLores in the East Nusa Tenggara. Many people went here to see the Komodo Nation Park, where the Komodo dragon live.

Mount Bromo

Mount Bromo is an active somma volcana and part of the Tengger mountains in East Java. With the height of 2.329 m, this mountain is one of the most visited tourist destinations in East Java. From a vantage point on Mount Penanjakan, visitors from around the world come to see the sunrise over Mount Bromo.

Sharks Teeth Beach

Shark Tooth Beach is located in Pegadungan Village, Kelumbayan, Lampung. This braach has a view of towering rock like a row of shark teeth. The location of this beach is far from the city center, that's why it's the Hidden Paradise in Lampung.

Mahakam River

Mahakam river is the 3rd longest and voluem discharge river in Borneo after Kapuas and Barito rivers. It's located in East Kalimantan. It flows 980 km from the districs of Long Apari in the highlands of Borneo to Makasaar Strait. The capital of East Kalimantan, Samarinda, lies aling 48 km from the river mouth

Derawan Islands

Derawan Islands are located in the East Kalimantan. It consits of 31 islands, most well known among these are Derawan, Kakaban, Maratua and Sangalaki. There are also numerous submerged reefs and islets. The islands is directly to Sulawesi Sea.

Lake Sentani

Lake Sentani is a tropical, shallow, and at low-altitude open lake located at the northeast extermity of Jayapura around 20 km from the Jayapura City. The locals considered this lake as the home of the rainbows, is part of the Cyclops Tricts Nature Reserve and contains several species of fish.

World Peace Gong

World Peace Gong is located in Ambon. This is the 35th World Peace Gong all around the world. In this gong, there are many flags from all the world. Beneath the monument, there is the museum which visitors can see photos that tell stories about Ambon's origin.

Lake Kapuas

Kapuas River is a river in West Kalimantan. This river is 1.143 km in length and the longest river of Indonesia and one of the world's longest island river. The delta of this river is located west-southwest of Pontianak. This river should be distinguished from another Kapuas River in the central Borneo, but flows to the south merging with Barito Rivers and discharging into the Java Sea
